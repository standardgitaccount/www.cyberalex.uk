About

By Alex • Published: 22 January 2026 • Updated: 22 January 2026

CyberAlex

Cyber-first, practical, systems-minded.

I’m Alex — a cyber security practitioner with a First Class BSc (Hons) in Computer Security, focused on translating complex technical risk into decisions that stand up in the real world.

I work in cyber security with a strong focus on evidence, clarity, and outcomes. I’m much less interested in how impressive something sounds and far more interested in whether it can be understood, tested, and relied upon when it actually matters.

My background is technical, but over time my focus has shifted toward how systems behave as a whole. I’m interested in how risk builds up, how decisions are made under pressure, and why security succeeds or fails in real organisations. A lot of that shift has come from seeing how often complexity and jargon get in the way of understanding what is really going on.

I value work that stands up to scrutiny. That means writing things clearly, being honest about assumptions, and being comfortable with review or challenge. Where something cannot be fully evidenced, I try to say so. I would rather be precise and incomplete than confident and wrong.

Progress matters to me, but not in a performative sense. I care about when something becomes true, when knowledge is consolidated, when work is validated, or when an outcome is awarded, rather than just the activity leading up to it. That way of thinking shapes how I structure my work, how I reflect on it, and how I present it. Finished, defensible outcomes carry more weight than ongoing effort.

I’m careful with personal data, attribution, and disclosure. Not because I’m secretive, but because I’m selective. In cyber security, judgement is shown as much by what you choose not to expose as by what you do. Redaction, minimisation, and context are part of doing the job properly.

Overall, my approach to cyber security is practical and unsentimental. I’m interested in what works, what fails, and why, and in building understanding that holds up in the real world rather than collapsing under it.
