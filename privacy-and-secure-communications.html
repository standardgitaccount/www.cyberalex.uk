Encryption Tool

By Alex • Published: 29 November 2025 • Updated: 29 November 2025

This page explains how CyberAlex handles privacy, how your data is treated when you visit the site, and how to contact me safely. The short version: the website does not track you, and legitimate people are always welcome to get in touch.

Normal, good-faith messages are encouraged — whether you’re an individual, student, organisation, or business. However, phishing attempts, abusive behaviour, malware delivery and similar misuse may be escalated and reported to the appropriate authorities. This page sets out those expectations clearly so genuine contact remains easy and safe for everyone.

At a Glance

  • No tracking, no analytics, no behavioural profiling.
  • Hosted on GitHub Pages — I do not see your IP or logs.
  • Client-side tools keep all input on your device.
  • Legitimate contact is welcomed and encouraged.
  • Malicious email may be forwarded for investigation.

Summary: Privacy is respected by default, and communication is open — provided it is legitimate and safe.

1. Privacy

No tracking, no analytics, no data collection

The CyberAlex website does not use cookies, analytics scripts, advertising trackers, fingerprinting technologies, or behavioural profiling. I do not collect, store, process, or monetise personal data from visitors.

Interactive tools on this site — such as the password tester, encryption utilities, and other client-side demos — run entirely in your browser. Your input never leaves your device, and no data is sent to me or any external service.

Hosted by GitHub Pages

The site is delivered via GitHub Pages. As part of normal web hosting, GitHub may collect standard server logs such as:

  • IP addresses,
  • browser type and user-agent details,
  • timestamps and basic request information.

These logs are used for operational and security purposes (for example, preventing abuse or diagnosing service issues). They are not shared with me, and I have no ability to identify individual visitors.

No third-party embeds or external tracking

CyberAlex does not embed third-party analytics, social media widgets, advertising networks, or remote JavaScript that could track you. Pages are intentionally kept simple and self-contained for privacy and performance.

Data retention

I do not retain or store any personal information about visitors. GitHub’s server logs are managed under their own policies. For details, see the GitHub Privacy Statement.

2. Communications

Please don’t be afraid to get in touch

I welcome legitimate contact from individuals, students, organisations and businesses. If you have a genuine question, idea, opportunity, request for help, or even constructive criticism, you are explicitly invited to contact me using the email address provided on the contact page.

In normal use, your email will simply be read and, where appropriate, replied to. I do not sell, rent or share genuine correspondence for marketing, analytics or profiling.

Secure email options

If you prefer a more secure channel, you can email me using a Tuta (tuta.com) account, which provides end-to-end encryption between Tuta users.

For higher-sensitivity discussions, I can also use modern post-quantum encryption or PGP. If you need that level of assurance, mention it in your initial message and we can agree a method that fits.

What you agree not to send

By choosing to reveal and use my email address, you agree that you will not send:

  • phishing or fraudulent messages,
  • malware, exploit links or malicious attachments,
  • harassing, threatening, or abusive content,
  • unsolicited bulk marketing or spam.

In other words: normal, good-faith contact is welcome; attempts to scam, abuse or attack are not.

If you try to phish, scam or abuse

Any email sent to the published contact address may be reviewed for signs of phishing, fraud, malware delivery or other abuse. If a message appears malicious or clearly outside normal expectations, I may retain it as evidence and forward it — including full headers and relevant links — to the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) and other appropriate partners.

The NCSC may analyse the email and any linked websites, and can work with providers and law enforcement to block or remove malicious infrastructure where appropriate. In practice, that means abusive or malicious email is not just ignored; it may be escalated beyond my inbox.

Legal protections and misuse

Emails sent to CyberAlex are subject to UK law, including but not limited to:

  • Protection from Harassment Act 1997 — prohibits harassment and malicious patterns of contact.
  • Communications Act 2003 (s.127) — covers grossly offensive, indecent, obscene or menacing messages.
  • Computer Misuse Act 1990 — prohibits unauthorised access attempts and malicious interference with systems.
  • Data Protection Act 2018 and UK GDPR — govern the handling of personal data in legitimate communications.
